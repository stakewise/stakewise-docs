# Where to Start: Create Your Vault

Now that you know what operating a Vault involves — and the opportunities it opens beyond native staking — let's create one.

Creating a Vault is done via the StakeWise UI and takes just a few minutes. If you're not sure about the exact parameters for your Vault, reach out to the [StakeWise team](mailto:info@stakewise.io) for personalized guidance. Once your Vault is created, you can set up the validator infrastructure and launch the Operator Service to begin your operator journey.

## Getting Started

1. Navigate to the [StakeWise App ↗](https://app.stakewise.io) and go to the **Operate** page
2. Click the **Create Vault** button to begin the setup process

## Step 1: Configure Vault Parameters

:::custom-warning[Permanent Settings]
Most of these settings **cannot be changed later**. Choose carefully before proceeding.
:::

Configure your Vault parameters:

- **Vault Capacity** – Set maximum ETH capacity (default: unlimited ∞). [Learn more →](/docs/vaults/customization#capacity)
- **Vault Fee** – Set the fee percent your Vault will charge from users staking rewards
- **Vault Token** – Enable tradeable ERC-20 token (requires name & symbol)
- **Private Vault** – Restrict to approved wallets only
- **Block List** – Prevent specific addresses from depositing

:::custom-info[Vault Fees Updates]
[Vault fees →](/docs/fees/intro#vault-fees) can be set during creation and updated later.
:::

## Step 2: Block Rewards Destination

Choose how block rewards are distributed. This is a **permanent decision**.

Most operators choose Smoothing Pool for stable returns, while Vault Escrow gives you full control but with volatile rewards. See [MEV Strategy →](/docs/vaults/customization#mev-strategy) for details.

## Step 3: Add Branding

Customize your Vault's appearance with name, description, and logo.

:::custom-notes[Branding Can Be Changed Later]
Unlike Vault parameters, you can update branding anytime via your Vault interface: press **Settings → Branding → Edit → Save**.
:::

## Step 4: Summary

Review all your settings before final submission. This is your last chance to verify everything is correct, such as name, type, MEV strategy, fees, and capacity.

Click **Create Vault** to submit your configuration and create the Vault.

## Step 5: Transaction Confirmation

Confirm the transaction in your wallet to deploy your Vault.
Once the transaction confirms, your Vault is live and visible on the **Operate** page! 🎉

## Next Steps

You can update branding, adjust fees, and assign roles on your Vault page in **Settings** and for private Vault, you can add/remove addresses authorized to deposit in **Vault access**.
To get your Vault verified for better visibility, reach out to us.

Your Vault is created but not yet operational — it needs node clients and the Operator Service to start accepting deposits and registering validators.
Follow the [Launch Operator Service V4 →](./operator-service/overview) guide to set everything up.
