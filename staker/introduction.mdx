# Introduction

[Staking](/docs/stakewise-protocol/understanding-liquid-staking) puts your ETH to work securing Ethereum, and you earn rewards for it.

## Why Stake with StakeWise?

- **Stay liquid** — When you stake, you receive [osETH](/docs/ostoken/intro), a liquid staking token you can trade or use in DeFi at any time.
- **No minimum required** — Stake any amount of ETH, no need for 32 ETH.
- **No technical skills needed** — Professional operators handle all validator infrastructure for you.
- **Keep earning** — The osETH:ETH exchange rate only increases over time as staking rewards accumulate, so your position grows in value automatically.
- **Built-in yield strategies** — [Boost](/docs/vaults/boost) uses your osETH as collateral on Aave to borrow more ETH and restake it, all with a single click. This has historically achieved 4–6% APY.
- **Battle-tested security** — StakeWise smart contracts secure over $1B in TVL and are regularly audited by leading security firms.

## How It Works

1. You deposit ETH into a [Vault](/docs/vaults/intro) — a smart contract that pools deposits and stakes them through validators.
2. You receive osETH in return — its exchange rate against ETH rises continuously as rewards flow in.
3. You can use your osETH freely — trade it, deploy it in DeFi, or convert it back to ETH whenever you want.

That's it. No lockups, no hardware, no complexity.
